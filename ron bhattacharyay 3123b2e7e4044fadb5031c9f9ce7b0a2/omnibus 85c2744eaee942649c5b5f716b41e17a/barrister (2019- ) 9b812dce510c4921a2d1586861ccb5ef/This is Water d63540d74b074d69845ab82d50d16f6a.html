Comments

I wish David Foster Wallace was alive to give this commencement address today, and after 15 years it's rough to see that we've gotten worse at this. I'm not good at this. I think for most of the smartest people in my generation, it's easy to write off sadness and struggle as a cost of intelligence and consciousness. If we're so smart, why's it so hard to be happy. I'm biased (DFW's one of my favorite authors), so take this with a grain of salt. I think "This is Water" is a non-judgmental playbook for happiness. It's one that's hard to practice, but an effort that's disproportionately rewarding. It's well written in DFW's uncanny humor, with a brevity that's refreshing to anyone who's read Infinite Jest. Reminds me a lot of Paul Graham.
